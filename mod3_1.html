Manila-Acapulco Galleon Trade 1565-1815. 

The Manila-Acapulco Galleon Trade is a trade route between Manila and Acapulco de Juarez that operated for 250 years. This was made possible by the usage of 2 vessels where one was outgoing and the other was incoming. The galleon trade benefited mostly privileged Spaniards such as the governors, consulado members and insulares. Chinese immigrants also benefited from the trade, they took advantage of the goods influx from Asia and sold them to the Spanish colonies in the Americas. Filipino labor was utilized in constructing galleons, from cutting wood,transporting wood and even planting the required materials. 

The galleon required coconuts as its coir (fiber from the outer husk of the coconut)is used for rigging and caulking the galleon. Abaca were also planted to be used for rigging materials. Sugar and sago palm fibers were also used to create ropes. As the demand for manpower for the galleon trade increased, the manpower from the agricultural sector were diverted to the trade. This decreased the production of food and also hamper the growth of the agricultural sector of the country. The country benefited from the trade through the arrival of various goods such as avocado, guava, papaya, pineapple, horses, and cattle.

Establishment of Economic Institutions under Spain

Taxation was introduced by the Spanish colonial government. It consisted of direct and indirect taxes. Direct taxes such as paying tribute or buwis can be paid in cash or as goods such as palay, tobacco, chickens, textiles, wax and any other goods produced in the region of the payer. 

When the 1570s arrived the tribute was now fixed to 8 reales and then raised to 15 reales where 1 real is equal to 12.50 centavos. Payment in goods were also set such as gold, blankets, cotton, rice and bells.The Filipinos were required to pay tribute of 10 reales, diezmos prediales a tithe of 1 real, town community chest of 1 real and sanctorum tax of 3 reales which totals to 15 reales. Samboangan or donativo de Zamboanga were also collected which costs 0.50 real or rice this was collected to finance the battle against the Moro and also to equip vessels that can protect the coastal areas.

Tax exemptions were granted to the descendants of Filipino chiefly class that helped in the pacification campaigns by the conquistadores.The bandala was also collected annually. It is an annual enforced sale or requisitioning of various goods. In 1884 paying tribute was replaced with cedula personal or personal identity paper. This tax is required to be paid by everyone over the age of eigtheen even foreigners.

Implementation of the Encomienda System

The Encomienda system is where a Spanish individual with utmost respect is given power and control over a specific area. There are two types of Encomiendas, the Royal or Crown and the private encomienda. The Royal encomienda is solely for the royal individuals which includes principal towns and ports. The private encomiendas were for the king’s proteges or individuals who helped in the conquest and pacification campaigns.

Encomenderos were authorized to collect the tribute payment yearly in cash. However, some of the encomendero charge a higher price violating the set limits.The encomendero also took advantage of the scarcity of goods and commodities. When a certain good was scarce they would demand for that certain good as payment.This made some of the Filipinos who are unable to pay escaped to the hills and were branded as tulisanes.

Rise of the Clase Media

The economic transformation of the Philippines led to the rise of the middle class also known as clase media. This was due to the increase of native entrepreneurs that caused an economic boom. Most of these individuals have atleast P50 in land taxes,decorated personnel, and school masters.

The indicators of social status in this century pertains to the size, materials used in construction and the location of the house in main street or calle real. Other symbols of an individual’s social status is the owning of livestock, sugarmills, imported furniture and tableware.

This rise of the middle class can be seen in Manila, where Intramuros contained more of the affluent people and the Spanish individuals. Extramuros is where the Filipino,Chinese and mestizo communities were located. Binondo, Ermita, San Jose de Trozo or Quiapo is where the mestizo and Filipino social elites were located. Tondo was home to the clase pobre or the lower class.
